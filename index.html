I am a MSc Candidate in Statistics at Simon Fraser University, advised by Dr. David Stenning. My research lies at the intersection of Bayesian inference, high-dimensional data analysis, and scalable samplers. I am broadly interested in developing principled statistical methods that are computationally efficient and theoretically grounded.

Before my Master's, I completed a BSc in Statistics at the University of Delhi, India. My interests include Bayesian inferencing and scallable sampling methods. I enjoy learning about faster and scallable algorithms with theoretical backing. Recently I've been learning about MCMC samplers, GPs and Deep GPs and am exploring their applications in astrostatistics.

Outside of research, I enjoy hiking, swimming, and also teaching. I have dabbled a bit into cubing and some logical puzzles. I enjoy live music and my favourite thing to do is to read on a sunny day.

Master's Thesis (Ongoing)

2025-26 · Working Paper
Adaptive Posterior Concentration Under Misspecified Priors

We study the asymptotic behaviour of the posterior distribution when the prior is misspecified with respect to the true data-generating mechanism. We establish minimax-optimal contraction rates and propose a data-driven correction scheme.

Bayesian Inference Minimax Theory Asymptotics

2025 · Working Paper
Sparse Bayesian Regression via Empirical Bayes Hyperparameter Selection

We develop a fully automatic empirical Bayes procedure for sparse regression in the normal means model. The method achieves near-oracle variable selection and estimation simultaneously, without cross-validation.
